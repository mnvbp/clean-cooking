# clean-cooking

This is the Github respoitory for my senior honors thesis project "Social and Economic Determinants Influencing the Health of Zambian Women and Children: Evidence from the Zambia DHS 2018" 

The Demographic and Heath Survey (DHS) Program funded by USAID collects nationally representative data on fertility, family planning, maternal and child health, gender, HIV/AIDS, malaria, and nutrition.

In my analysis, I mainly utilized STATA to clean, merge, and transform the various recodes of the 2018 Zambia surey data (Birth, Children, Household, and Women). I further utilized STATA to run multiple linear regressions, tabulations, and pair-wire coorelation coefficents for the independent and dependent variables. 

I utilized Python to compute the distance between village clusters and various mining operations in Zambia utilizing the Haversine formula to approximate the distance between two coordiates. This aspect of the data analysis did not end up making it to my final thesis.

Utilized R to develop plots for my thesis defense.

I am unable to publically provide the Recode data for this project due to agreement with DHS, however if you are affiliated with an academic institution, you are able to request access to the data on the DHS website (Zambia 2018 STATA files) https://dhsprogram.com/

Abstract:
"In Zambia, the proportion of individuals using unclean fuels is increasing, despite the downward global trend exhibited by most other countries. While previous studies including Zambia have analyzed the link between household air pollution and under-five mortality in sub-Saharan Africa, there is a severe lack of in-depth analysis of the effects of unclean fuel on several outcome variables. This study utilizes data from the 2018 Demographic and Health Survey of Zambia to elucidate how socioeconomic factors influence several health indicators. Five indicators were chosen for analysis using logistic regression: “low birthweight,” “ever had a terminated pregnancy,” “ever had a cough in the last two weeks,” ”anemia level,” and “ever told by a doctor or health worker that have raised blood pressure or hypertension. Of the socioeconomic factors tested, individuals were significantly more likely to be anemic when belonging to households in the poorest wealth quintile (when compared to the median), households with a female head, or if their gender was male. Early pregnancy termination was significantly linked to outdoor cooking, smoking within the household, and wealth quintile. High blood pressure was significantly linked with unclean fuel use, outdoor cooking, wealth quintile, and urban-rural divide. This study provides a preliminary analysis of the effects of several socioeconomic factors on the outcome variables of interest. These results provide a preliminary basis for investigations into the health of households in Zambia."
